Brown Sugar Chicken



Description

This is a quick and easy recipe that could be done on a weeknight. It is an amazing balance
of sweet, savory, tangy, and spicy flavors. It is definitely something different and esasy to
throw into rotation. It is so good, even a picky eater might love it! This has become a weekly
staple in our home and I recommend giving it a try!

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 6 chicken tenders
  • 2 tbsps. flour
  • 1 tsp. EACH paprika, garlic powder, onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp. EACH chipotle chili pepper, ground cumin, salt, black pepper
  • 2 tbsps. butter
  • 2 tbsps. olive oil
  • White vinegar for cleaning the chicken

Brown Sugar Sauce

  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp. lemon juice
  • 1 tsp. dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp corn starch
  • 1/2 tsp. EACH (dried) parsley, oregano, thyme.
  • Red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Slice the chicken tenders in half and place in a mixing bowl.
  2. Fill the bowl with white vinegar to a little above the chicken. Swirl the chicken around
    and then let it soak in the vinegar for 3 minutes.
  3. While the chicken soaks, mix the flour and all the chicken seasoning in a small bowl.
    Reserve 1 tbsp. of the mixture and put aside for the sauce.
  4. Melt the 2 tbsps. of butter and the 2 tbsps. of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  5. Drain your chicken in a colander and rinse the bowl out, then place the chicken back in
    the bowl. Pour your flour mixture in and mix it all up, then add the coated pieces to your
    frying pan. Fry the chicken until golden brown, about 4 minutes on each side. Transfer the
    chicken to a plate and don't wipe out the skillet.
  6. Mix the chicken broth with the cornstarch in a small bowl and add to the skillet, followed by
    all the sauce ingredients, including the reserved tbsp. of the flour mixture. Bring the sauce
    to a simmer until it thickens to the desired consistency. Add more lemon juice for tangier/less
    sweet taste if desired. Add the red chili flakes and adjust to your desired spice heat level.
  7. Add chicken back to skillet and and spoon some sauce over top. Garnish with fresh or dried parsley
    and serve with mashed potatoes or rice. If the chicken is too sweet, garnish with lemon juice.
